Crazy Things I've Done in WWII Re-Enacting

My Units:

    1st SS Panzer Division "LSSAH" 2nd Panzer Division

    ARTILLERY

      PaK 40

      2nd Panzer Division

      In the years before I became old enough to carry a rifle, I helped with the cannon crews of various guns. I first served with the crew of a PaK 40 75mm anti-tank gun, destroyed a Sherman tank at my very first re-enactment. At first I simply helped carry shells, but years later I got the chance to be the loader.

      leI.G. 18

      2nd Panzer Division

      On many occasions I also served on the crew of a 75mm leichtes Infanteriegeschütz, or "light infantry gun." I have both loaded and fired the gun.

      PaK 36

      2nd Panzer Division

      The last type of towed gun I have served on so far is the 37mm PaK 36 anti-tank gun. I have also both loaded and fired this infamously ineffective (but still fun) weapon.

    VEHICLES

      Jagdpanzer 38t "Hetzer"

      2nd Panzer Division

      The Hetzer tank destroyer is a very special vehicle to me. My father, grandfather, and uncle all served together on a Hetzer in St. Louis for many years back in the day. Now, I have had the chance to serve in a Hetzer as well, as the loader and gunner (simultaneously). My father has also since served as the commander of the vehicle many times now.

      Marder III Ausf. H

      LSSAH

      Most recently, I have had the incredible privelege to serve as the gunner of a newly restored Marder III H tank destroyer onwed by Bob Graebe in the 1st SS, and once again, my father serves as the commander. I'm looking very forward to future events in which I can serve on this wonderful vehicle.

      Sdkfz 250

      2nd Panzer Division

      I recently got the chance to learn how to drive an Sdkfz 250 armored half-track. Just another thing to add to the list, and another skill I hope to be able to expand on some time.

      Sdkfz 250 w/ PaK 36

      LSSAH

      With the SS, I also once was given the opportunity to operate the .50 cal blank-adapted 37mm gun on one of their half-tracks. I had one of the most awesome battle experiences of my career pumping out shot after shot into the enemy, blowing away a multitude of allied light-skinned vehicles in front of me. It was incredible.
